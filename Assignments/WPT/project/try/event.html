Events / Tools

Discover our events and tools-sharing hub, where you can connect, learn, and collaborate! Attend workshops, webinars, and meet-ups designed to inspire and equip you with valuable skills and insights. Urban Spaces Design Guide

A comprehensive guide for community organizers, urban planners, and public space enthusiasts interested in revitalizing urban areas. This guide includes templates for community surveys, checklists for safe and accessible street design, and best practices for creating pedestrian-friendly zones. Available for borrowing at our tools-sharing library, it’s ideal for anyone looking to transform local public spaces into vibrant, inclusive areas.

Last updated November 1, 2024

Community Gardening Handbook

A practical book for novice and experienced gardeners alike, focusing on creating sustainable community gardens. This handbook provides tips on soil management, crop rotation, and native plant integration, with detailed sections on organizing neighborhood garden initiatives. Perfect for communities looking to start or improve shared green spaces, this resource is available for loan to support community-led environmental projects.

Last updated November 1, 2024

Event Planning & Community Engagement Handbook

This handbook is a go-to guide for organizing events that build community and foster engagement. It includes templates for invitations, planning timelines, volunteer management tips, and checklists for successful community events. Designed for community organizers, nonprofits, and local councils, this handbook is available to borrow and can help make your next event a success.

Last updated November 1, 2024

Green Infrastructure for Public Spaces: A Design Guide

A book focusing on sustainable landscaping and eco-friendly design for parks, streetscapes, and public plazas. This guide covers rain gardens, urban forestry, and permeable surfaces, offering actionable ideas for communities looking to incorporate green infrastructure. Available in our tools library, it’s perfect for anyone planning to integrate environmental solutions into public space projects.
